Work

For over a decade, I have been dedicated to youth development and data science education in Africa, and I have spent most part of my career contributing to this area of capacity building locally, regionally and internationally. I am the Vice-President of Global Engagement and Public Relations of the LISA 2020 Global Network. In 2013, I was the only candidate selected out of 108 applicants from 34 developing countries for a Google Fellowship to travel to the United States as a Statistics and Data Science Ambassador to begin the LISA 2020 Global Network. The LISA 2020 Global Network is a USAID sponsored program that builds statistics and data science capacity in developing countries by creating a robust network of statistics and data science collaboration laboratories. These laboratories are engines for development by training the next generation of collaborative statisticians and data scientists; collaborating at the intersections of data-driven development with researchers, data producers, policy actors and decision-makers to make positive impact on society; and teaching short courses and workshops to improve statistical skills and data literacy widely (www.lisa2020.org).

As the first LISA 2020 World Fellow, I returned to Nigeria from the United States after my one year training, to pioneer a new frontier of data science education that is currently being replicated in about twenty developing countries. I created the first LISA 2020 Data Science Laboratory in Africa in 2014. Since then, I have charted a new wave in data science and statistics education in developing countries by helping institutions to move from theory to practice in data science knowledge dissemination, especially among youths. As the LISA 2020 Ambassador to Africa, I helped to coordinate and engage several networks of data science and statistics laboratories scattered across countries in Africa and other developing countries. I am the lead editor/author of the widely read book titled “Promoting Statistical Practice and Collaboration in Developing Countries.” I have presented papers in several conferences in five continents of the world. I was among the 25 African Scholars selected as part of the Cohort 4 Affiliate Members of the prestigious African Academy of Sciences in 2018. I was part of the five panellists, alongside the then President of the American Statistical Association-David Morganstein, who discussed on “Mentoring” at the Conference for Statistical Practice (CSP-2014), Tampa, Florida in 2014. I am an Elected Member of the International Statistical Institute, Netherlands, a Fellow of the African Scientific Institute, USA and an International Research Fellow at the Institute of Mathematics, Statistics and Scientific Computing, University of Campinas, Brazil. I have consulted for several organizations including the International Institute for Tropical Agriculture (IITA), Nigeria and Human Sciences Research Council (HSRC), Cape Town, South Africa on their Data Management and Machine Learning projects respectively. I was appointed as Honorary Professor and PhD Advisor at Global Humanistic University Curacao in 2020. I have extensive experience in promoting statistics and data science education among Africans with little or no access to such education. I have been blazing new trails in what is still largely an uncharted territory in Africa. I am also the founder of ADA Global Academy which offers free training and research opportunities to young African Scholars. I was the first Nigerian to serve on the council of the International Society for Business and Industrial Statistics (ISBIS). I organized the first ISBIS/World Bank sponsored capacity building workshop in Africa. I was appointed as a country coordinator (Nigeria) of the International Statistical Literacy Program of the International Statistical Institute (ISI) in 2017. Through these programmes, I have trained and mentored thousands of young scholars in Africa and beyond. I have published over sixty articles in local and international journals in the area of applied statistics, data science, machine learning and statistics education.
